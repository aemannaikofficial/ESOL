SCQF stands for Scottish Credit and Qualifications Framework. It provides a common understanding for describing qualification levels. The ESOL awards with LEB Secure English Language Test are SCQF credit rated and sit within the SCQF database.

ESOL Awards with LEB Secure English Language Test have been SCQF Credit Rated

mapped to SCQF Levels and are broadly comparable with other ESOL qualifications awarded.

English levels

  • A1 – Beginner
  • A2 – Elementary
  • B1 – Intermediate
  • B2 – Lower Advanced
  • C1 – Advanced
  • C2 – Proficient

B2 – Lower Advanced

  • At the B2 level, learners have advanced beyond the intermediate stage and have acquired a solid command of the language that allows them to engage in more complex and nuanced communication. This level represents a substantial leap in language skills, enabling learners to navigate a wide range of social, academic, and professional situations with confidence.
AT THIS LEVEL, INDIVIDUALS CAN:
  • Listening: Understand extended conversations and discussions, even when they involve abstract or complex topics.
  • Participate in in-depth conversations and debates, expressing opinions and viewpoints with clarity and support.
  • Understand longer and more complex texts, including articles, essays, and reports.
  • Produce well-structured and coherent texts on various topics, presenting arguments and viewpoints effectively.
